## Design Context

### Users
TermFleet is used by developers and operators supervising many terminal-backed workstreams on a Linux desktop. Their primary job is to identify the project or session that needs attention, switch to it immediately, and understand its state without reading the terminal transcript. Cockpit labels must also remain legible to a non-technical observer watching progress.

### Brand Personality
Refined, industrial, calm. The interface should create confidence through precise hierarchy and honest state, not decorative futurism or visual intensity.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark, terminal-first operational utility with Rubik for interface text and monospace reserved for terminal content. Use neutral workbench surfaces, restrained density, one operational accent, compact icon actions, and progressive disclosure. Avoid floating card stacks, chip-heavy chrome, nested panels, glows, verbose metadata, and competing scroll regions.

### Design Principles
- Keep the active terminal and command entry visually dominant; navigation is supporting instrumentation.
- Show one scan line per project or session by default, then reveal task details and secondary actions on selection or focus.
- Give each narrow panel one clear scrolling owner and one primary action.
- Preserve keyboard access and honest status while removing duplicated labels and passive metadata.
- Prefer quiet fills, dividers, and negative space over borders, shadows, or nested cards.
